Pennywort

Assam is the home of a variety of medicinal herbs and plants. About 300 types of medicinal herbs and plants are known to exist in abundance in the State. Pennywort, scientifically known as Centella asiatica, is a herbaceous plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine systems, particularly in Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), and traditional Southeast Asian medicine. Here are some potential medicinal uses of pennywort.

Uses:

Pennywort is often used to improve cognitive function and enhance memory. It is believed to have neuroprotective properties, supporting brain health and enhancing mental clarity and focus. Some research suggests that pennywort may help improve cognitive function in individuals with cognitive impairment or age-related memory decline. : Pennywort has been traditionally used topically to promote wound healing and skin regeneration. It is believed to stimulate collagen production and improve circulation to the skin, aiding in the repair of wounds, cuts, and abrasions. Pennywort extracts or ointments are sometimes applied to wounds to facilitate healing and reduce scarring. Pennywort possesses anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce inflammation and swelling in various parts of the body. It is used to alleviate symptoms of inflammatory conditions such as arthritis, rheumatism, and skin disorders like eczema and psoriasis. Pennywort is used in traditional medicine to improve venous circulation and treat conditions such as varicose veins and chronic venous insufficiency. It is believed to strengthen blood vessels, improve blood flow, and reduce swelling and discomfort associated with poor circulation.
