Juniperus indica

Uniperus indica, the black juniper, is a juniper native to high-altitude climates in the Himalaya, mostly in Himachal Pradesh, occurring in India, Nepal, Bhutan and China. It is a shrub growing to 50–200 centimetres (20–79 in) tall, with largely horizontal branching. The leaves are dark gray-green, dimorphic, with adult plants having mostly scale-like leaves 1–3 millimetres.

Uses:

Juniper berries or extract of the plant has traditionally been used as diuretic, anti-arthritis, anti-diabetes, antiseptic as well as for the treatment of gastrointestinal and autoimmune disorders. The biofunctions of Juniperus indica Bertol including its anticancer potential, have not been fully explored. The fruits are eaten as a cure for fevers and headaches.
