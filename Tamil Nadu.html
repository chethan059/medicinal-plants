Adhatoda vasica

Adathoda, scientifically known as Adhatoda vasica, is a medicinal plant commonly found in Tamil Nadu and other parts of India. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is also known by various names such as Malabar Nut, Vasaka, or Justicia adhatoda. In traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda and Siddha, Adathoda has been used for its therapeutic properties, particularly in the treatment of respiratory conditions.

Uses:

Adathoda is primarily known for its efficacy in treating respiratory issues such as cough, bronchitis, asthma, and other breathing difficulties. The leaves of Adathoda contain alkaloids and other compounds that help to loosen phlegm and mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel.
