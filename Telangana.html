NEEM

Neem (Azadirachta indica) is a versatile tree native to the Indian subcontinent, including the region of Telangana. It has been an integral part of traditional Indian medicine, particularly Ayurveda, for centuries. Neem is known for its wide range of medicinal properties and has been referred to as the "village pharmacy" due to its numerous health benefits

Uses:

Neem is used to treat various ailments due to its antibacterial, antifungal antiviral, antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. It is used internally and externally for conditions such as skin disorders (eczema, acne, psoriasis), digestive issues, fever, malaria, diabetes, oral health problems, and more.

TULSI

Tulsi in Hindi or Tulasi in Sanskrit (holy basil in English) is a highly revered culinary and medicinal aromatic herb from the family Lamiaceae that is indigenous to the Indian subcontinent and been used within Ayurvedic medicine more than 3000 years.
