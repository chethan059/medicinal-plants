BAEL

Bael, scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, is a medicinal tree species native to India and commonly found in regions like Goa. It belongs to the Rutaceae family. Bael holds significant importance in traditional Indian medicine systems such as Ayurveda, where it has been used for centuries due to its various therapeutic properties. Bael possesses antimicrobial properties, making it effective against a wide range of bacteria, fungi, and parasites. It helps in combating infections in the gastrointestinal tract and promoting gut health.

Uses:

Bael is renowned for its digestive properties. Both the fruit and leaves of the bael tree are used to treat various digestive disorders such as indigestion, constipation, diarrhea, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Bael is rich in antioxidants, including flavonoids, phenolic compounds, and vitamin C, which help neutralize free radicals and protect cells from oxidative damage. This property contributes to its overall health benefits and may help prevent various chronic diseases. Bael is sometimes used externally for its skin-healing properties.
