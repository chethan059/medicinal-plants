Cardamom

Cardamom, scientifically known as Elettaria cardamomum, is a spice native to the Indian subcontinent and is widely cultivated in countries such as India, Sri Lanka, Guatemala, and Tanzania. It is highly valued for its unique flavor, aromatic fragrance, and various medicinal properties.

Uses:

Cardamom is known for its carminative properties, which help to relieve gas, bloating, and stomach cramps. It is used to alleviate coughs, congestion, and respiratory infections, promoting clearer breathing and respiratory comfort.

Tribulus terrestris

Tribulus terrestris, commonly known as puncture vine or gokshura in Ayurvedic medicine, is a plant species native to warm temperate and tropical regions of Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia. It belongs to the Zygophyllaceae family and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine systems for its various medicinal properties.

Uses:

It is used to support urinary health and may help alleviate symptoms of urinary tract infections and kidney stones. Tribulus terrestris is believed to enhance immune function and promote overall health and well-being. It may help strengthen the body's defenses against infections and diseases by stimulating the production of immune cells and enhancing immune response.
